# Chimera Ants

## Topic: Chimera Ants
Type: Species and arc threat
Aliases: Chimera Ant, Chimera Ants, Kimera Ant
Related: Queen, Meruem, Royal Guards, NGL, East Gorteau, Netero, Komugi

Summary:
Chimera Ants are creatures that pass traits from consumed organisms to their offspring. In Hunter x Hunter, a Chimera Ant Queen produces increasingly intelligent and powerful ants, eventually giving birth to Meruem.

Key facts:
- The Queen consumes humans and other creatures to produce stronger offspring.
- Meruem is the King.
- Neferpitou, Shaiapouf, and Menthuthuyoupi are the Royal Guards.
- The threat grows in NGL and later East Gorteau.
- The palace invasion is the major operation against Meruem's forces.

## Origin and escalation
The crisis begins when a Chimera Ant Queen reaches a stage where eating humans creates offspring with human intelligence, emotion, and Nen potential. What starts as a biological threat becomes a national and then global-level danger.

## NGL and East Gorteau
The early spread happens in NGL, where the ants grow in secrecy. Later the conflict moves into East Gorteau, where Meruem's rise turns the arc into a struggle involving the Hunter Association, state power, and elite Nen users.

## Royal Guards
Neferpitou, Shaiapouf, and Menthuthuyoupi are Meruem's Royal Guards. Pitou represents instinct and terrifying raw talent, Pouf represents obsession and manipulation, and Youpi represents explosive combat power and transformation.

## Meruem and Komugi
Meruem's bond with Komugi changes his understanding of strength, value, and humanity.

## Netero vs Meruem
Netero fights Meruem using 100-Type Guanyin Bodhisattva. The fight contrasts human discipline with Meruem's overwhelming power.

## Palace invasion
The Hunters launch a carefully timed palace invasion to separate Meruem from the Royal Guards and stop a larger catastrophe. The operation depends on coordination, sacrifice, and the very different abilities of Netero's team.

## Gon vs Pitou
Gon's confrontation with Pitou is driven by grief and rage over Kite. It shows the danger of extreme Nen vows.

## Themes
The arc explores evolution, humanity, power, sacrifice, cruelty, compassion, and identity.
